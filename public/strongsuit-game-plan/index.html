What I'm seeing from the outside

Five things holding the account back

A quick outside read of the live ads and site. All fixable, and none of it requires more budget.

Every click lands on a generic homepage. No practice-area benefits above the fold, so high-intent visitors bounce before they see why it matters to them.

Creative is stale and off-brand. Old purple assets are still running months after the rebrand: same ads, same look, no new angles tested.

Search budget is leaking on low-intent queries. Generic and "free"-style terms attract tire-kickers, not law firms shopping for a real solution.

Lead quality is diluted. Instant forms without verification let spam and non-serious submissions through to the sales team.

No consistent message tying it together. Taglines describe the product; nothing tells a family law or PI firm what changes for them.

The core insight

Law firms buy four outcomes

I run acquisition for law firms every day. Every purchase decision ladders up to the same handful of levers.

Grow revenue

More cases signed and serviced without adding headcount. "Double your team's capacity without hiring" is a message partners actually stop for.

Move cases faster

Faster case turnover means revenue collected sooner. Settling weeks faster isn't a feature, it's cash flow, and firm owners know it.

Help more people

The same team serves more clients. For firms that measure impact as well as income, expanding who they can take on is a message that lands.

Spend time on higher-value work

Automate the busywork so attorneys and paralegals focus on strategy and clients, the work that actually wins cases and grows the firm.

Every ad, landing page, and webinar in this plan is built to prove one of these outcomes for a specific practice area, in the firm's own language.

Landing pages

Fix the destination in two steps

Stop sending paid traffic to a generic homepage. We prove the lift with a cleaner page first, then get specific by practice area.

Step 1 · Prove it

A clearer single landing page

Test one focused page with sharper messaging and the important information above the fold: what StrongSuit does, who it's for, and the outcome, without making visitors scroll or navigate to find it. Establish a real conversion baseline.

Step 2 · Get specific

Practice-area pages

Once the cleaner page beats the homepage, split into dedicated pages per practice area, each mirroring its campaign's promise.

Family law: capacity and caseload. Personal injury: case velocity and volume. Litigation: output and prep time.

Low lift on your side: I design and spec every page (or hand your developers production-ready code) and can build and test variants in days, not weeks.
Lead quality

Force quality first, then reduce friction

Sales time is the most expensive thing in the funnel. We protect it before we chase volume.

Turn on

Phone (OTP) verification

On Meta instant forms. Spam and non-serious submissions won't verify a phone number. Sales only sees real prospects.

Turn off

Flexible form delivery

Meta quietly rearranges or drops form questions. We lock the form to high-intent so the qualifying questions always run.

Expect

A short-term cost trade

Cost per lead may rise ~10–20% while verification is on. Once a quality baseline is set, we remove friction and bring CPL back down.

Google Ads

Stop paying for the wrong searches

The fastest win in the account: reallocate spend from low-intent queries to the terms already converting efficiently.

Tighten the structure

Move to exact match with disciplined negative-keyword work. Clear message → clear landing page. No display or PMax noise for this stage.

Simple success test

Can we double impression share on the efficient terms while holding CPA under target? That's the scale path hiding in the account today.

Creative & video

Video is the biggest lever in legal

Static ads don't move law firms. Outcome-driven video does. It's how I acquire legal clients today, and it's the gap competitors are exploiting.

Refresh to the new brand

Retire legacy creative, align everything with current positioning, and cycle fresh angles every 6 to 12 weeks, testing messages, not color swaps.

Outcome-based video

30–60 second clips per practice area: "How litigators are doubling output," "How PI firms close cases months faster." I provide the scripts.

Customer proof

Interview your happiest power-user firms, turn their numbers into testimonials, case studies, and webinar material. If filming in-house is hard, I'll arrange production.

Channel roadmap

Meta and Google have plenty of headroom once the foundations are fixed. Everything else waits until the machine is proven.

Deliberately parked. Revisit for thought-leadership and brand awareness only once Meta and Google are compounding.
