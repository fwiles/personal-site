- We scaled a PI Meta campaign from $15k/month to $80k/month in spend.
- That campaign generated 55 signed cases last month.
- We’re aiming to hit $100k/month soon.

We’ve also been generating signed cases consistently for several PI, SSDI, and Workers’ Comp clients spending between $10k to $20k per month.

On the creative side, we’re producing more ads than ever. At this point, some smaller accounts cannot test everything we create.

**The biggest changes I’ve made to the team:**
- Creative managers can now see signed case data down to the ad level.
- We started reviewing performance for every client each month.
- We connected Meta campaign data to Claude so the team can pull reports in minutes.
- The team can now see signed case performance for every ad across every account.
- We built a database of top-performing scripts and creative instead of starting from scratch for every client.

Our creative team was already good at making ads.

They just needed better data to know what was actually working.

We also standardized our media buying process and shifted our focus from raw lead cost to signed case cost.

Before this, media buyers were optimizing accounts differently and often making decisions based only on cheap leads.

That sometimes hurt backend performance.

One of the biggest improvements was setting up Meta CRM events so campaigns could optimize toward deeper funnel actions instead of just lead cost.

It took time to set up, but it has become non-negotiable for every new client.

For now, we’ve also moved many campaigns from landing pages to Instant Forms so we can launch faster and move quicker as a lean team.
