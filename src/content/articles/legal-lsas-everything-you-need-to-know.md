# Legal LSAs: Everything You Need to Know

If you run a law firm and do not use Local Services Ads (LSAs), you may be missing good leads.
I have spent years running paid search and social campaigns for law firms.
This guide explains how LSAs work, why they matter, and what to do when they stop spending.

---

## Why LSAs Deserve Your Attention

![Legal LSA Search Example](/article-images/lsa-search-results-1.jpg)

LSAs show at the top of Google search results for many legal searches.
On mobile, they are often the first thing a potential client sees.
You pay per call, not per click.

Here is a common legal search results page:

1. LSA ads
2. One or two PPC ads
3. Map pack (occasionally boosted by a PPC campaign)
4. Organic search results
5. PPC ads again

LSAs sit above everything else.
That matters in a crowded market.

---

## What to Know Before Getting Started

LSAs are simple to set up.
Google built the platform so firms can manage it without much help.
Once it is running, it usually does not need much work.

Before you start, keep these points in mind:

- **Don't overpay for management.** Most of the work is in setup. A one-time setup fee can make sense. A big monthly fee usually does not.
- **You need a verified Google Business Profile.** You cannot skip this.
- **If you've never advertised before, start here.** LSAs are a better first step than regular PPC for many firms.
- **Budget realistically.** For personal injury, start around $1,000 per week. For many other practice areas, start around $375 to $750 per week.

## The Three Common Issues

Almost every firm I work with has seen at least one of these problems:

### 1. New Accounts That Won't Spend

New LSA accounts sometimes do not spend at all.
This can happen even when the budget is high enough and the profile is complete.
It does not always mean your setup is wrong.

### 2. Accounts That Randomly Stop Spending

An active account can stop spending with no clear reason.
Sometimes you can find the cause.
Sometimes you cannot.
The platform is inconsistent, and Google support often gives different answers.

### 3. Accounts That Hit a Spending Ceiling

Some accounts hit a ceiling.
They will not spend more, even if you raise the budget.
This is common.

**The main point:** Do not rely only on LSAs for leads.
Use other channels too.

---

## Known Reasons LSAs Stop Spending

![Legal LSA Response Time Example](/article-images/LSA-Respond-Time-Badge.jpg)

When an account goes quiet, the cause is often one of three things:

### Missed Calls

The LSA system penalizes firms that miss calls.
Only run ads when your intake team is answering the phone.
An answering service can help, but it often leads to fewer signed clients.

### Slow Message Response Times

If you turn on message leads without a 24/7 chat service, your profile may show "Responds within 1 day."
Other firms may show "Responds within 1 minute."
That gap can hurt performance and spend.
If you do not have real-time chat, do not turn on message leads.

### Negative Reviews

If your firm has only a few reviews, one or two bad ones can drop your rating fast.
That can hurt your LSA performance.
You need to keep asking happy clients for reviews.

## What to Do When Your LSA Stops Spending

If your account goes dark, try these steps over about two weeks.
Do them in order, not all at once:

1. Increase your budget
2. Switch to manual bidding and set it to $1,000 (or $500 or less for non-PI practices)
3. Upload new images to your profile
4. Turn the account off over the weekend; turn it back on Monday morning
5. Enable "Direct Business Search"
6. Enable "Get leads looking for general law help"

None of these steps is guaranteed.
But I have seen each one work.
If you try them all and the account still does not move, make a new one.

---

## Creating a New LSA Account After the Old One Fails

This process is simple:

1. Create a new LSA account and complete the full verification process.
2. **Transfer your reviews.** Call LSA support and ask them to disconnect your reviews from the old account. Hang up, call again, and ask them to connect those reviews to your new account.

Google often gives more spend to new accounts.
That is why a fresh account can fix problems that will not go away.

## LSA Profile Settings: What to Turn On and What to Avoid

### Do Not Enable These Features

- **"Get leads looking for general law help"** This setting can send you leads for many legal issues you do not handle. Avoid it unless you cover almost everything.
- **"Direct Business Search"** This can make you overpay for calls from people already looking for your firm. Run a branded PPC campaign instead.
- **"Message leads"** This can hurt your account if you do not have a 24/7 chat service handling messages.

### Scheduling

Only run ads when intake is staffed and answering calls.
Answering services are fine, but they often waste budget and convert worse.

### Your Headshot Matters More Than You Think

Your profile photo can affect clicks.
Background color can matter too.
If you can, test different photos and different attorneys.
Do not assume your current photo is the best one.

---

## How to Break Through a Spending Ceiling

Raising your budget alone usually does not fix a spending ceiling.
I have seen accounts with very large budgets spend nothing.

The most reliable way I have found to raise spend past a ceiling is to turn on message leads with a 24/7 chat service.
The goal is to earn the "Responds within 1 minute" badge.
Across multiple accounts, I have seen this lift spend by about 20%.

This costs money.
But if your firm wants to scale LSA spend, it is one of the best levers I have seen.

---

## Final Thoughts

LSAs can be one of the most cost-effective paid channels for law firms.
You pay per call.
They show in a strong spot on the page.
They also do not need much ongoing work.

But the platform is inconsistent.
If you treat it like a set-it-and-forget-it channel, you can get caught off guard when spend drops to zero.
Know the common problems before they happen.
Use more than one lead source.
When something breaks, work through the steps before starting over.
