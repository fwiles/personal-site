In the world of law firm marketing, Meta ads are often misunderstood. Many firms assume Facebook and Instagram ads "don't work," when in reality the bigger issue is usually the creative, the intake process, or the way results are being tracked.

On the **Grow With Kara** show, I sat down with Kara Prior to break down what's actually working right now in Meta advertising for law firms. From scroll-stopping hooks to better follow-up systems, I shared practical insights for firms that want better leads, better cases, and better ROI from paid social.

## Key Takeaways

- Why the first 3-5 seconds of a Meta ad often determine whether it succeeds or fails
- Why "ugly," story-driven ads usually outperform polished lawyer commercials
- Why attorneys should appear in their own ads to improve trust, familiarity, and long-term brand lift
- Why firms should focus on signed case cost, not just cost per lead
- How weak intake follow-up often becomes the real bottleneck in campaign performance
- Why broad targeting tends to outperform narrow geographic targeting on Meta
- Why feeding CRM outcomes back into Meta is becoming essential for better optimization

For firms running Meta ads, or considering them, this conversation offers a clear look at what separates underperforming campaigns from the ones that actually drive revenue.
